= Import Ansible content from Ansible Galaxy by using {ProjectWebUI}

[role="_abstract"]
You can import Ansible content from Ansible Galaxy into {Project} by using {ProjectWebUI}.
Select Ansible Galaxy as content source if you want to import Ansible content from the Ansible Galaxy, from a resource that implements the Ansible Galaxy API, from a Pulp instance, or from a content view.

.Prerequisites
* Your {Project} account has a role that grants the `create_ansible_content`, `view_ansible_content`, and `view_organizations` permissions.

.Procedure
. In the {ProjectWebUI}, navigate to *Ansible* > *Content*.
. Click *Import Ansible content*.
. Select *Ansible Galaxy* as source and click *Next*.
. In the *Identifier* field, enter the namespace and name of the Ansible content separated by a dot.
. In the *Source* field, enter the URL of your Ansible Galaxy.
By default, it uses https://galaxy.ansible.com/[galaxy.ansible.com].
. In the *Versions* field, you can add versions to limit the synchronization to one or multiple versions.
Click the `+` sign or press enter after each version.
. Click *Add Ansible collection to batch*.
+
Optionally, you can repeat the steps to add another Ansible collection.
. Click *Next*.
. Review your content.
You can click *Delete* to remove an Ansible collection from the batch.
. Click *Finish* to import Ansible content.

.Verification
. In the {ProjectWebUI}, navigate to *Ansible* > *Content*.
. For your imported Ansible content, click the *arrow* symbol to view the list of imported versions.
. Verify that {Project} contains all selected Ansible content and versions.
