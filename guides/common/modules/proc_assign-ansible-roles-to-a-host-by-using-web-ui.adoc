= Assign Ansible roles to a host by using {ProjectWebUI}

[role="_abstract"]
You can assign Ansible roles to your host by using {ProjectWebUI}.
Ansible roles define the Ansible content that is used to configure your host.

.Prerequisites
* Your {Project} account has a role that grants the `create_ansible_lifecycle_environment_paths`, `edit_ansible_lifecycle_environment_paths`, `view_ansible_content`, `view_ansible_lifecycle_environment_paths`, `view_ansible_lifecycle_environments`, and `view_organizations` permissions.
* Your {Project} contains an Ansible lifecycle environment with an Ansible Execution Environment and Ansible content.

.Procedure
. In the {ProjectWebUI}, navigate to *Hosts* > *All Hosts*.
. Select your host.
. On the *Ansible Director* tab, select the *Content* tab.
. From the list of Ansible roles, select a version of an Ansible role and click on the *Right Arrow* icon.
. Click the *Save* icon to assign Ansible roles to your host.

.Verification
. In the {ProjectWebUI}, navigate to *Hosts* > *All Hosts*.
. Select your host.
. On the *Ansible Director* tab, select the *Content* tab.
. Verify that {Project} shows your newly assigned Ansible roles.
